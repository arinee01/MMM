Disclaimer

The purpose of this web site is to explore various types of typographic and layout style for museum pages, as an end-of-course project for the "Information Modeling and Web Technologies" course of the Master Degree in Digital Humanities and Digital Knowledge of the University of Bologna, under prof. Fabio Vitali.

The documents contained in this web site have been selected for their length and complexity from various cultural and historical sources related to mystical traditions, protective symbols, and magical practices. Their publication here is not intended to be an alternative or replace their original locations:

  • Ancient Egyptian Scarab Amulets

    Historical artifacts and cultural practices from ancient Egyptian civilization

    British Museum - Scarab Collection
  • Norse Protective Symbols

    Aegishjalmr and other protective runes from Viking Age traditions

    National Museum of Denmark - Viking Religion & Magic
  • Medieval Christian Amulets

    St. Benedict medals and protective symbols from medieval Christian traditions

    Museum Digital Baden-Württemberg - Benedictus-Medaille
  • Japanese Omamori Charms

    Traditional protective charms from Japanese Shinto and Buddhist practices

    National Folk Museum of Korea - East Asian Shamanic Practices
  • Mesoamerican Protective Symbols

    Ojo de Venado and other protective amulets from pre-Columbian cultures

    National Museum of Anthropology, Mexico - INAH Collections
  • African Spiritual Objects

    Bantu fetishes and traditional African protective practices

    Royal Museum for Central Africa - Nkisi Collection
  • Korean Ritual Tools

    Traditional Korean shamanic and protective implements

    National Folk Museum of Korea - Shamanic Rituals
  • Slavic Folk Traditions

    Protection dolls and Slavic magical practices

    The Ukrainian Museum NYC - Motanka Dolls
  • Turkish Evil Eye Amulets

    Traditional protective symbols from Turkish and Mediterranean cultures

    Istanbul Museums - Glass & Ethnographic Collections
  • Universal Healing Crystals

    Rose quartz and other crystals used in various healing traditions

    Natural History Museum London - Mineralogy Collections
  • Prehistoric Protective Stones

    Ancient black stones and protective minerals from various cultures

    British Museum - Prehistoric Britain Gallery
  • Modern Digital Artifacts

    NFT QR codes and digital protective symbols from contemporary culture

    LACMA - Digital Art Collection
  • Gaming Culture Objects

    RPG amulets and virtual protective items from gaming traditions

    Victoria & Albert Museum - Videogames Exhibition
